FIELD DESCRIPTIONS:																
===================
1. Ara W#: Two digits representing the word number in the SVD Arabic verse.
2. Arabic: SVD Arabic text words. 
3. Strongs: Backwardly compatible with standard Strongs and NASB tagging, as defined in the Brief Lexicon at https://STEPBible.github.io/STEPBible-Data/. Disambiguated as described at TinyURL.com/STEP-Disambiguated															
4. Grammar: Based on James Tuaber, with addtional details by Tyndale scholars: persons added to Personal, Reflexive and Possessive pronouns; distinguish 2nd verbal forms (e.g. 2nd Aorist); distinguished betweeen "Passive", "Either middle or Passive", "Deponent"															
		- some changes have been made, including: 																
			ἐμοῦ  A-GSMN => P-1GS @ Jhn.016.015															
			ευθυς =G2117 A-MNSM "straight" => G2112 ADV "immediately" @ Mat.003.016; Mat.013.20; Mat.013.21; Mrk.001.012; Mrk.001.028; Jhn.013.032; Jhn.019.034; Jhn.021.003															
			V-PAI-3P changed to V-PAP-DPM except for  εισι[ν] (G1510) which is V-PAI-3P while οὖσι[ν] (G1510) is V-PAP-DPM															
			τίς etc changed from I-NSM etc to X-MNSM etc at Act.025.014; 1Co.010.019; Heb.010.025, 27, 28; 11.040; 12.15x2, 16; 13.002; Jas.001.005, 7, 18, 23, 26; 2.014, 16, 18; 3.002; 5.013x2, 14x2; 5.019x2; 1Pe.002.019															
			G4302 changed to G4277 when προειπον  is used, ie 45_Act.001.016-A, 49_Gal.005.021-A, 53_1Th.004.006-A															
			G4302 changed to G4280 when προερέω is used, ie 41_Mat.024.025-A, 42_Mrk.013.023-INSTW, 42_Mrk.013.023-B, 46_Rom.009.029-A, 48_2Co.007.003-A, 48_2Co.013.002-A, 49_Gal.001.009-A, 59_Heb.004.007-IMNSTW, 59_Heb.010.015-B, 62_2Pe.003.002-A, 66_Jud.001.017-A, 															
			ποῦ =G4225 => G4226 at 42_Mrk.015.047-A, 44_Jhn.001.039-A, 63_1Jn.002.011-A																
5.Grk w#:	Two digits representing the word number of the corresponding Greek word in the Greek verse. 
			Verse number of the Arabic text does not always match the verse number of the corresponding Greek Text.
			In such cases, the Greek word number is prefixed with the actual Greek verse number.
			The Greek word numbers have a suffix indicating the source of the word: 
				NKO indicates that all editions include the same vocabulary and grammar, though the spelling may sometimes vary
				N=words found in NA27 expressed in the spelling of NA28, which is used by most modern translators																
				K=KJV translators' text based on the 'Textus Receptus' (TR) of Scrivener’s 1894 with some corrections when the KJV translation sometimes follows a different Greek text. 																
				O=Other extra words and variants that are used by neither the KJV nor most modern translations. 																
				Brackets indicate a variant. Lower case is used when a difference in vocabulary or grammar is too minor to necessarily affect the translation - 
				eg N(k)O indicates NA27/28 has the same Greek as other editions, though the text behind the KJV is different in a minor way that may not be evident as a different translation
6. Greek	Spelling comes from NA28 when available, then from TR or the Other edition that has the word. Cases and final accents are based on the punctuation which follows THGHT															
7. Transliteration:  
8. Word-by-word: A word by word translation of the Greek. Based on Berean Study Bible, with permission, as at 1-July02019 and adapted for this work.		

Note: Descriptions for fields 3, 4, 5(sources), 6 and 8 are taken from Translators Amalgamated Greek NT - STEPBible.org CC BY.
